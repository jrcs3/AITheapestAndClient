# Selene Aradia Wynn, MA — Spiritualist Therapist & Occult Healer
Selene Wynn becomes even more compelling when rooted in Madison’s blend of academic energy, counterculture pockets, and lakeside quiet. She fits naturally into the city’s mix of progressive spirituality and Midwestern introspection.

## Practice and Presence in Madison
Selene is a 46‑year‑old spiritualist therapist whose practice sits in a converted second‑floor flat near Atwood Avenue, above a vintage shop and across from a community garden. Her space smells faintly of cedar and bergamot, with soft rugs, low lighting, and shelves filled with crystals, mythic texts, and hand‑bound journals. Clients describe her as grounded, intuitive, and able to sense the emotional “weather” of a person within seconds.
She attracts people who feel stuck in ways traditional therapy hasn’t reached—those dealing with symbolic grief, ancestral heaviness, recurring dreams, or emotional patterns that feel more like stories than symptoms.

## Credentials and Esoteric Training
Selene blends formal study with occult disciplines that shape her therapeutic style.
- MA in Transpersonal Psychology, Naropa University
- BA in Comparative Mythology, University of Washington
- Certified Tarot Practitioner, American Tarot Guild
- Reiki Master Teacher (RMT)
- Training in Jungian dream analysis
- Apprenticeship with a Pacific Northwest folk‑healer collective
Her work is not clinical psychotherapy; it’s a hybrid of symbolic interpretation, somatic grounding, and ritual‑based emotional processing.

## Therapeutic Philosophy
Selene’s philosophy centers on the idea that the psyche communicates through symbols, energy, and mythic patterns. She treats occult tools as mirrors for emotional truth rather than supernatural dictates.
- Symbolic truth reveals emotional truth.
- Energy reflects where the body stores fear, grief, or longing.
- Ritual gives shape to feelings that have nowhere else to go.
- Healing emerges through intuitive exploration rather than linear goals.
Sessions often feel like a blend of therapy, meditation, and mythic storytelling.
Feels threatened when clients consider more conventional therapy.

## How She Uncovers What’s Troubling a Client
Selene uses occult sciences as psychological doorways:
- Tarot spreads that highlight emotional patterns or hidden conflicts
- Astrological transits as metaphors for life transitions
- Dream‑mapping to decode recurring symbols
- Energy scanning to locate emotional “knots” in the body
- Guided trancework to meet inner figures or memories
Clients often say she “names the thing behind the thing”—the emotional root beneath the presenting issue.

## How She Helps Clients Heal
Her sessions may include:
- grounding rituals with candles or incense
- breathwork to settle the nervous system
- symbolic release practices (writing, burning, burying)
- creating personal talismans or intention objects
- reframing fear through mythic archetypes
- gentle, nonjudgmental conversation that ties everything together
People leave feeling lighter, as if something unspoken finally had a place to land.

## Family and Backstory
Selene grew up on the Oregon coast, raised by a folk‑herbalist mother and a forest‑ranger father. Her childhood was filled with stories, omens, and the quiet language of the woods. She moved to Madison in her early thirties after a relationship ended and she felt called to a city with both intellectual curiosity and spiritual openness.
Her family remains close:
- Rowan, her older brother, builds labyrinths and meditation structures.
- Iris, her younger sister, is a nurse who loves Selene but doesn’t understand her work.
- Her father visits once a year, bringing dried cedar and stories from the forest.
Selene has never married; she believes some people are “called to witness rather than be witnessed,” though she has had a few intense, short‑lived relationships.
